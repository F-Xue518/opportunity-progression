# MEDDPICC Framework Reference

Shared knowledge base for MEDDPICC methodology.

## Element Definitions

| Code | Element | Definition |
|------|---------|------------|
| M | Metrics | Quantifiable measures of value AWS solutions provide |
| E | Economic Buyer | Person with overall authority (veto power) in the buying decision |
| DC | Decision Criteria | Criteria by which the purchase decision will be judged |
| DP | Decision Process | Steps the buyer follows to make a decision |
| P | Paper Process | Process to sign/commit once the business decision is made |
| I | Implicate the Pain | Encouraging the customer to discuss pain and business implications |
| CH | Champion | Person with power, influence, credibility who sells when you're not there |
| CP | Competition | Any person, vendor, or initiative competing for same funds, including doing nothing |

## Categories

- **Business Driven:** I, M, E, CH
- **Process Driven:** CP, DC, DP, P

## Scoring

- Total possible: 100 points across 8 elements (each element 12-13 points, 3-4 statements scored 1-5)
- ≥80% = On Track | 60-79% = Needs Improvement | <60% = At Risk

## Element Interdependency Matrix — MEDDPICC (Strategic tier, 8 elements)

| Element | Directly Related | Impact |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| M | I, E | Metrics quantify pain; align with EB priorities |
| E | DC, DP | EB influences DC details and DP |
| DC | I, CP | DC must address pain; differentiates against competition |
| DP | E, CH | EB influences DP; Champion guides through DP |
| P | E, DP | PP links to EB and DP |
| I | M, DC | Pain generates Metrics; influences DC |
| CH | I, CP | Champion guides pain ID; helps understand competition |
| CP | DC, I | Competition influences DC; pain quantifies differentiated value |

## Element Interdependency Matrix — EDDIC (Simple tier, 5 elements)

Use this matrix when analyzing Simple-tier opportunities. **Do not** reuse the MEDDPICC matrix with M/P/CH simply "ignored" — that produces misleading relationships (e.g., DP in MEDDPICC depends on CH, but in EDDIC it depends solely on E).

| Element | Directly Related | Impact |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| E (Economic Buyer) | DC, DP | EB influences Decision Criteria details and Decision Process |
| DC (Decision Criteria) | I, CP | DC must address pain; differentiates against competitive/alternative solutions |
| DP (Decision Process) | E | EB influences DP. (Internal supporter/Champion plays a guiding role in DP, but is **not a scored element or direct dependency** in EDDIC — see Role Discipline below) |
| I (Implicate the Pain) | DC | Pain quantification produces metrics; pain influences DC formation |
| CP (Competition) | DC, I | Competition influences DC; pain quantifies differentiated value |

**E is the true hub in EDDIC.** With Champion removed as an independent element, E becomes the only upstream dependency for DP and a direct influencer of DC. Expect a low E score to cascade into DP and DC far more sharply than the equivalent situation in MEDDPICC.

**Note on terminology:** The Competition element is abbreviated **CP** across this skill, the MEDDPICC and EDDIC Scorecard templates, and all supporting documentation. External MEDDPICC training materials sometimes use "CO" (first two letters of Competition); treat "CO" as a synonymous legacy label if encountered in user input, but always emit **CP** in output. Scorecard parsing must accept either label.

## Categories — EDDIC

- **Business Driven:** I, E
- **Process Driven:** DC, DP, CP

## Scoring — EDDIC

- 5 elements scored, per-element structure follows same template (3-4 statements × 1-5 scale)
- Thresholds same as MEDDPICC: ≥80% On Track | 60-79% Needs Improvement | <60% At Risk

## Role Discipline

- Only **Economic Buyer** and **Champion** are key MEDDPICC roles (Strategic tier only)
- In **EDDIC (Simple tier)**, Champion is **not a scored element**. An internal supporter may guide DP, but this is a relationship health signal only — treat as Phase 5 sanity check, not a scoring dimension
- All other contacts are classified as **Coaches** — helpful, but cannot close the deal
